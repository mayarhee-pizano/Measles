History of Measles

Measles was first recognized in the 9th century by a Persian docter. It soon became clear that it was an infectious disease. In 1912 it was required by all labratories and doctors to report all measles cases.The first decade after 1912 6000 fatalities were caused by measles. In 1954 the first measles vaccince was created when some scientists isolated the measles virus in a sick patients blood. In 1963 the vaccine was legalized and used in the United States. The measles vaccine drastically reduced the number of people getting measles and limited the number of measles outbreaks.
