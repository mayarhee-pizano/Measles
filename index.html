History of Measles

Measles was first recognized in the 9th century by a Persian docter. In 1912 it was required by all labratories and hospitals to report every measles case.The first decade after 1912 6000 fatalities were caused by measles. In 1954 the first measles vaccince was created. Some scientists isolated the measles virus in a sick patients blood and created the vaccine. In 1963 the vaccine was legalized and utilized in the United States. The measles vaccine drastically reduced the number of people getting measles and limited the number of measles outbreaks.
